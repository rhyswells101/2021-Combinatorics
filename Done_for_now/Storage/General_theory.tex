\begin{definition}
For $G$ a group, a principal homogeneous $G$-space is a set $X$ with a free transitive action $\beta$.
\end{definition}

A free and transitive (a regular) action means that for all $x_1,x_2 \in X$ there exists a unique $g \in G$ such that $\beta(g,x_1)=x_2$.

\begin{definition} Let $G$ be a group and $S,T$ be sets. A subset of $S\subseteq T$ is a complete set of representatives for the action $G$ on $T$ if and only if there exists a unique $s \in S$ such that $x=g(s)$ for some $g \in G$ and $x \in T$.
\end{definition}
